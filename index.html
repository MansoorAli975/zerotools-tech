Why an AI chatbot matters

A normal website shows information. An AI chatbot turns your website from a brochure into a guide by helping visitors ask questions, share useful details, and move toward the next step.

A website as a brochure

  • Visitors read the page and leave
  • Questions wait for a later reply
  • Enquiries often arrive incomplete
  • After hours, the website goes quiet
  • Visitors decide the next step alone

A website as a guide

  • Visitors get answers while they are still interested
  • Common questions are answered instantly
  • Useful details are collected early
  • After hours, the chatbot keeps responding
  • Visitors are pointed toward a clear next step

Frequently Asked Questions

Quick answers about Zero Tools, chatbot setup, pricing, and how the process works.

What does Zero Tools do?

Is Zero Tools chatbot software or a done-for-you service?

Zero Tools is a done-for-you chatbot setup service.

How is Zero Tools different from low-cost DIY chatbot tools?

Low-cost DIY tools usually give you software to configure yourself.

Zero Tools helps build the chatbot structure for your business, including conversation flow, content setup, lead capture, testing, and launch preparation.

Can’t a chatbot just scan my website automatically?

Website scanning can help with basic answers, but it is not enough for many businesses.

A useful chatbot also needs structure: what to ask, what to collect, how to guide visitors, and when to move a lead forward.

Do I need technical knowledge to get a chatbot?

No. You do not need technical knowledge.

Zero Tools handles the chatbot setup process and guides you through the information needed to prepare your website chatbot.

What is the difference between Basic, Advanced, and Pro?

Basic answers common website questions and captures basic enquiries.

Advanced guides visitors through more organized conversations and collects better enquiry details.

Pro handles different visitor situations, includes a Visitor Follow-up Tracker, flags priority enquiries, and includes a 14-day post-launch review.

What is the difference between Pro English and Pro Arabic?

Pro English and Pro Arabic provide the same Pro capability level, including Smart Visitor Handling, structured follow-up, Priority Enquiry Alert, and the Visitor Follow-up Tracker.

Pro English is delivered in English. Pro Arabic is delivered in Arabic through its own dedicated right-to-left chatbot deployment.

Businesses that need both languages use Pro English & Pro Arabic, which consists of two dedicated chatbot deployments rather than one bilingual chatbot.

Does Pro include automatic follow-up?

No. Pro includes a Visitor Follow-up Tracker, Priority Enquiry Alert, and Simple Follow-up Protocol to help organize manual follow-up.

Automatic follow-up messages, reminders, campaigns, or workflow automation require separate review based on the client’s tools and process.

Does Zero Tools currently offer WhatsApp chatbots?

No. Zero Tools is currently focused on website chatbot systems.

WhatsApp is not currently offered as a standard product and is not included in Basic, Advanced, Pro English, Pro Arabic, or Pro English & Pro Arabic.

What is the Formal Implementation Brief?

The Formal Implementation Brief is the first-stage document used for all Zero Tools packages and Business Automation Review requests.

It helps us understand your business, goals, requested direction, language needs, general scope, add-on interests, and any high-level workflow requirements before confirming feasibility, scope, and pricing.

Does submitting the Formal Implementation Brief mean my project is accepted?

No. Submitting the brief begins the review process.

Zero Tools may confirm the direction, ask for clarification, refine or simplify the scope, recommend a different setup, provide pricing, postpone the request, or decline work that is not suitable or technically feasible.

Do I complete Project Setup Details at the same time?

No. Complete the Formal Implementation Brief first.

Project Setup Details is sent later only after the first-stage review, the proposed scope and commercial direction have been discussed, and you decide to proceed.

It then collects the approved content, configuration, routing, technical, workflow, testing, and launch information needed for implementation.
